Partech Impact Fund
Growth-stage impact equity filling the “missing middle” for European B2B tech scale-ups
Impact: 4/5 Financial: 4/5
The Problem

European impact-native tech companies face a financing desert. At €10M+ revenue, they’re too mature for VC but too small for PE or infrastructure funds. These are companies with real traction, real impact — and nowhere to go for growth capital. The result: promising climate, agtech, and healthtech scale-ups either stall, get acquired early, or dilute themselves chasing the wrong investors.

The Solution

Partech Impact Fund is a €300M growth fund targeting exactly this gap. They write €15–40M checks into European B2B tech companies where the core product intrinsically addresses an environmental or social challenge. Sectors include clean production, climate tech, agtech, mobility, and digital health.

The fund sits within Partech Partners — a 40-year-old firm with ~€3B AUM, 21 unicorns, and offices across Europe, Africa, and the US. Current portfolio includes SustainCERT (carbon verification), Gireve (EV charging interoperability), xFarm (precision agriculture), Makersite (sustainable product design), and FYLD (AI for infrastructure).

Impact Framework

ABC Classification: C — Contribute to Solutions. Every portfolio company must have impact embedded in the core product — not a CSR bolt-on. The fund explicitly targets SDGs 7, 9, 11, 12, 13, and 15.

  • What: Each company mapped to specific outcomes (tCO2e avoided, MWh renewable, hectares of sustainable farming)
  • Who: Farmers, EV drivers, infrastructure workers, manufacturers, carbon markets — depending on company
  • How Much: Custom KPIs with baselines and targets, monitored quarterly
  • Contribution: Capital additionality (genuine funding gap) + active operational value creation
  • Risk: Assessed per OPIM framework — execution, evidence, external, stakeholder risk

Full alignment across all 9 Operating Principles for Impact Management. Annual independent verification.

What Makes Them Different

50% of carried interest is tied to achieving impact KPIs. This is one of the most aggressive GP–LP alignment mechanisms in European private markets. If they don’t hit impact targets, they don’t get half their carry. That’s not marketing — it’s structural.

Thesis Alignment

What I like about Partech Impact is the honesty of the thesis. The “missing middle” is real — I’ve seen it from the operator side. Growth-stage impact companies struggle to find investors who understand both the commercial opportunity and the impact logic. Partech’s 50% impact-linked carry is the thing that sets them apart. Most funds talk about impact; this one literally stakes half its economics on it. The first-time team is a risk, but the platform mitigates it. I’d want to see how the remaining 60% deploys and what exits look like before full conviction, but this is exactly the kind of fund I want to exist.
