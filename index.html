My Investment Framework
How I evaluate impact investment opportunities

My Philosophy

I believe impact investing should target market-rate returns. This isn't about accepting lower returns for "doing good"‚Äîit's about identifying companies where commercial success and positive impact are the same thing.

According to GIIN's 2024 report, 75% of impact investors target market-rate returns, and 94% of impact investments meet or exceed financial expectations. Impact and returns aren't a trade-off‚Äîthey're aligned when you pick the right companies.

Impact Assessment: Five Dimensions

I use the Impact Management Project's Five Dimensions framework‚Äîthe industry standard for measuring impact. For each company, I assess:

WHAT What outcome is the company creating? How important is it to the stakeholders experiencing it?
WHO Who experiences the outcome? How underserved are they in relation to this outcome?
HOW MUCH How many people experience it? How significant is the change? How long does it last?
CONTRIBUTION What is the company's contribution? Would this happen anyway without this investment?
RISK What is the risk that the impact doesn't happen as expected?

SDG Alignment

Every company I evaluate is mapped to the UN Sustainable Development Goals. This provides a common language for describing impact and helps compare opportunities across sectors.

I focus primarily on:

  • SDG 3: Good Health & Well-being
  • SDG 9: Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure
  • SDG 12: Responsible Consumption & Production
  • SDG 13: Climate Action

Financial Assessment

Impact without financial sustainability doesn't scale. For every company, I evaluate:

  • Market Size: TAM, SAM, SOM‚Äîis this market large enough to support a venture-scale outcome?
  • Business Model: Unit economics, path to profitability, recurring revenue potential
  • Competitive Moat: IP, network effects, switching costs, regulatory advantages
  • Team: Domain expertise, track record, ability to execute
  • Exit Path: Realistic acquirers, timeline to liquidity, comparable exits

The Integration Question

The most important question I ask: Is impact core to the business model, or bolted on?

The best impact investments are ones where financial success and impact success are the same thing. If the company makes money, it creates impact. If it creates impact, it makes money. When these are misaligned, something eventually breaks.

"If the business model succeeds, does impact automatically follow? If the company fails financially, what happens to the impact?"

Decision Framework

Every company must pass these gates:

  • Is impact core to the business model? Must be YES
  • Is there a path to financial sustainability? Must be YES
  • Does the team have relevant expertise? Must be YES
  • Is the market large enough? Must be YES
  • Is there a realistic exit path? Must be YES
