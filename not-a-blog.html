11 March 2026 Research

Gut bacteria might be why we lose our memory as we age

Researchers found that a specific gut bacteria (Parabacteroides goldsteinii) increases as mice get older—and it's linked to memory loss. When young mice lived with old mice and picked up their gut microbes, they started forgetting things too. The wild part? When scientists killed off this bacteria in old mice, their memory improved to levels comparable with young, healthy mice. The gut literally talks to the brain through sensory nerves, and this bacteria disrupts that conversation.

Why this matters to me: Gut health is deeply personal for me. I've dealt with gut issues for years—the inflammation, the impact on my period, my energy, even my self-esteem on bad days. So when I see research showing the gut might also affect how clearly we think and remember, it clicks. We're only scratching the surface of understanding this gut-brain connection, and honestly, it makes me hopeful. If a bacteria can cause cognitive decline, maybe we can actually do something about it.

Source: Edward Chen, "Memory loss is fuelled by gut microbes in ageing mice", Nature, 11 March 2026
